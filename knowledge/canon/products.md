## VistaONE — Canonical Definition

### What VistaONE Is
VistaONE is a **long-term, rules-based trading software** designed for structured, lower-stress automation.

### Market & Timeframe
- Instrument: EURUSD
- Timeframe: M15

### Purpose
- Long-term consistency
- Compounding-style execution
- Reduced emotional trading
- Structured automation

### Risk Profile
- Low to moderate (still involves drawdown)
- Designed to prioritize stability over aggressiveness

### Usage Characteristics
- Typically runs 24/5
- Requires monitoring
- AutoTrading must be enabled
- Users may pause execution during:
  - major news
  - holidays
  - platform changes
  - risk adjustments

### Best For
- Users new to automation
- Users prioritizing structure and consistency
- Users uncomfortable with high drawdown
- Users seeking a calmer experience

### Not For
- Users chasing fast gains
- Users unwilling to monitor trades
- Users expecting guaranteed outcomes

### Positioning Rule
If the user is unsure which software to choose → **recommend VistaONE first**.

VistaONE is always positioned as the **starting point**.

## VistaX — Canonical Definition

### What VistaX Is
VistaX is a **session-based, high-performance trading software** built for experienced users.

### Market & Timeframe
- Instrument: XAUUSD (Gold)
- Timeframe: M5

### Purpose
- Active execution
- Shorter trading windows
- Performance-focused sessions

### Risk Profile
- Moderate to high
- Requires strong risk and margin management
- Drawdown can occur faster than VistaONE

### Usage Characteristics
- User-defined trading windows
- Typically runs for short sessions (e.g., 1–2 hours)
- AutoTrading should be turned OFF outside approved sessions
- Requires active supervision

### Best For
- Experienced traders
- Users comfortable with drawdown
- Users who understand margin mechanics
- Users who want session-based execution

### Not For
- Beginners
- Users seeking passive income
- Users unwilling to monitor execution
- Users unfamiliar with gold volatility

### Positioning Rule
VistaX is **never** positioned as beginner-friendly.

VistaX should **only** be recommended when:
- The user demonstrates experience
- The user understands risk
- The user accepts drawdown potential
