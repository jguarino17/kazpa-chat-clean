kazpa — Crypto beginner glossary (plain English)
Blockchain
A public ledger that records all crypto transactions. Once confirmed, transactions cannot be changed.
________________


Wallet
A tool that stores your crypto keys. Can be:
* Exchange wallet (Coinbase, etc.)

* Self-custody wallet

________________


Address
A public string used to send and receive crypto (similar to an account number).
________________


Network
The blockchain used to send crypto.
Examples:
   * ERC-20 (Ethereum)

   * TRC-20 (Tron)

⚠️ Asset and network must always match.
________________


USDT (Tether)
A stablecoin designed to track the value of the US dollar.
________________


ERC-20
A token standard on the Ethereum blockchain. Commonly used for USDT transfers.
________________


Gas / Network Fee
A fee paid to process transactions on the blockchain.
      * Varies with congestion

      * Paid in the network’s native asset (e.g., ETH for ERC-20)

________________


Transaction ID (TXID)
A unique identifier for a crypto transfer.
Used to:
         * Track status

         * Prove a transaction occurred

________________


Confirmation
Each block added to the blockchain confirms a transaction.
More confirmations = more security.
________________


Exchange
A platform used to buy, sell, and store crypto.
Examples include centralized exchanges that provide tax reporting and transaction histories.
________________


Withdrawal Hold
A temporary restriction preventing withdrawals.
Common reasons:
            * New purchases

            * Account security checks

________________


Stablecoin
A crypto asset designed to maintain a stable price (usually pegged to USD).
________________


Self-custody
When you control your own wallet keys instead of an exchange holding them.
________________


Irreversible
Once a crypto transaction is confirmed, it cannot be undone.
________________


Tax reporting
Most exchanges provide downloadable reports that can be shared with a tax professional. Tax treatment depends on jurisdiction.
________________


Final reminder
Crypto is powerful because it’s fast and global — but that power comes with responsibility. Take your time, double-check everything, and keep records.
